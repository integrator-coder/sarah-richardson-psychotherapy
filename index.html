Meet Sarah

The choice to start therapy is significant and courageous, and I'm honoured to be part of that process with you.

My approach is grounded in empathy and curiosity. I believe it is important for therapeutic work to be rooted in mutual respect, transparency, and integrity. I place a lot of emphasis on building and maintaining a meaningful and trusting relationship with each client. It is my hope that together, we'll set supportive therapeutic goals, build tools to manage distress, gain new perspectives, and strengthen your overall well-being.

Before training as a psychotherapist, I spent 15 years working as a Registered Massage Therapist (RMT). This experience has shaped how I understand the connection between body and mind — how stress, trauma, and emotional pain are often held physically, not just thought about. It's part of why I bring particular attention and care to clients living with trauma, chronic pain, women's health issues (endometriosis, perimenopause, and menopause) and why I see healing as something that involves the whole person, not just the mind in isolation. The relationship that our minds and bodies have with each other is a passion of mine.

I have experience supporting individuals who have encountered trauma or abuse, whether in childhood or adulthood, including PTSD and Complex PTSD (C-PTSD). I understand the complexity of trauma and adapt my approach to each client's unique needs, pace, and readiness. My background in a range of therapeutic methods allows me to offer flexible, effective support suited to your specific situation — whether you're working through anxiety, grief, relationship difficulties, addictions, disordered eating, women's health experiences, neurodivergence, or chronic pain.

How I Can Help

I offer individual therapy for adults navigating a wide range of life's challenges. My work is client-centred, trauma-informed, and adaptive to support your needs.

Trauma, PTSD & Complex PTSD

Whether trauma occurred in childhood or adulthood, its effects can shape how safe we feel in our bodies, our relationships, and our sense of self. I offer a paced, trauma-informed approach that respects your readiness.

Anxiety

Anxiety can show up as constant worry, physical tension, racing thoughts, or a sense of being on edge. We'll work together to understand what's driving it and build practical tools to help you feel more grounded.

Grief & Loss

Grief doesn't follow a straight line, and there's no "right" way to move through it. I offer a compassionate space to process loss — of a person, a relationship, an identity, or a life you imagined.

Relationship Issues

Patterns in our relationships often connect back to earlier experiences and attachment styles. We'll explore what's not working, build communication and boundary-setting skills, and work toward healthier, more fulfilling connections.

Addiction

Addiction is often a response to pain, not a personal failing. I approach this work with compassion and without judgment, helping you understand the role addiction has played and build sustainable paths forward.

Eating Disorders

Disordered eating is rarely just about food — it often connects to control, identity, trauma, or emotional regulation. I provide a supportive, non-judgmental space to explore these connections and work toward a healthier relationship with yourself.

Neurodivergence

Whether you're exploring a new diagnosis, have long known you think and experience the world differently, or are supporting a neurodivergent family member, therapy can be a space to work with your neurodivergence rather than against it. I take a strengths-based, affirming approach to help you understand your own patterns, navigate a world not built with you in mind, and process the anxiety, masking fatigue, or relationship strain that can come along with it.

Chronic Pain

Living with chronic pain affects far more than the body — it touches mood, identity, relationships, and daily functioning. Drawing on my 15 years of experience as a Registered Massage Therapist alongside my psychotherapy training, I offer a uniquely body-informed approach to the emotional side of chronic pain.

Women's Health

Women's health experiences — including endometriosis, perimenopause, decisions around whether to have children, and fertility struggles — carry emotional weight that often goes unacknowledged. These experiences can bring up grief, uncertainty, identity shifts, and complicated feelings that deserve a dedicated space to process. I offer compassionate, informed support for women navigating these transitions, drawing on my background in both body-based and psychotherapeutic work to hold the physical and emotional sides of these experiences together.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes — I offer both virtual and in-person sessions to fit your needs and comfort level.

What can I expect in a consultation?

The consultation provides an opportunity for us to get to know each other. We'll talk about what brings you to therapy, your goals, and what you're hoping to get out of our work together. There's no pressure to share more than feels comfortable.

We can:

  • Talk about what's bringing you to therapy right now
  • Share a bit of your history and context, as much as feels comfortable
  • Discuss your goals and what you're hoping to get out of our work together
  • Ask any questions you have about my approach, confidentiality, or how sessions work
  • Get a feel for whether we're a good fit to work together going forward

There's no set agenda beyond that — this session is about starting to build trust and making sure you feel comfortable moving forward.

How long are sessions, and how much do they cost?

Sessions are 60 minutes and cost $160. 75 and 90 minute sessions are also available. A sliding scale/low-fee waitlist is available — please email to inquire.

What is your cancellation policy?

There is a 24-hour cancellation requirement. If an appointment is late-cancelled or missed, clients are charged the full session fee.

Are you currently accepting new clients?

Yes, I am currently accepting new clients for both virtual and in-person sessions.
