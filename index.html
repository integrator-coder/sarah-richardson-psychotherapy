About Sarah

I believe that therapy is a collaborative journey—one where you feel heard, understood, and empowered to make meaningful changes in your life. My approach is grounded in empathy, curiosity, and respect for your individual experience.

Whether you're struggling with anxiety, navigating a difficult relationship, processing grief, or simply feeling stuck, I'm here to help you explore what's happening beneath the surface and discover new ways forward.

I draw from evidence-based approaches including Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT), and mindfulness practices, tailoring our work together to what feels right for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

If you're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or simply curious about making positive changes in your life, therapy can help. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from counselling. Many people seek therapy for personal growth, better relationships, or support during transitions. The free consultation is a great way to explore whether we're a good fit.

What can I expect in our first session?

In our first session, we'll discuss what brings you to therapy, your goals, and any immediate concerns. I'll ask questions to better understand your background and current situation. This is also your chance to ask me questions and get a feel for my approach. There's no pressure—we'll move at your pace.

How long does therapy take?

This varies greatly depending on your goals and needs. Some clients find what they're looking for in a few months, while others prefer longer-term support. We'll check in regularly about your progress and adjust as needed. You're always in control of the pace and duration of our work together.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes! I offer secure video sessions for clients across Ontario. Virtual therapy provides flexibility and comfort, allowing you to attend sessions from wherever you feel most at ease. I also offer in-person sessions in Ottawa for those who prefer face-to-face meetings.

What are your fees and do you accept insurance?

My fee is $160 per 60-minute session. I provide receipts that can be submitted to extended health insurance plans that cover Registered Psychotherapists. Many plans in Ontario cover psychotherapy— I recommend checking with your provider about your coverage. I also offer a limited number of sliding scale spots for those experiencing financial hardship.

Is what we talk about confidential?

Yes. Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. What you share stays between us, with a few legal exceptions (such as if there's risk of harm to yourself or others, or if records are subpoenaed by a court). I'll discuss these limits in our first session so you feel informed and safe.
